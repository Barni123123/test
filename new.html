So on International Coffee Day, sit back with a cup of your favorite coffee option and learn 13 facts about coffee you might not know.

Coffee is actually cherry

The beans you make coffee from are actually roasted seeds from a fruit called the coffee cherry. If you bite into such a cherry, inside you will find two flattened seeds.

About 5% of the world's coffee, according to the US National Coffee Association, contains an oval bean called "peaberry" because the outside of the seed looks like a pea. In Spanish, such grains are called "caracolillo" ("little snail").

Some people "eat" coffee

People have been drinking coffee for centuries, but some people prefer to eat it.

Some companies also use leftover coffee cherries to make flour. It is used to make cakes, bread, chocolate, sauces and other products. However, they are not always enjoyed as coffee: depending on the type, floral, citrus or fruity notes will be felt.

Coffee made from stool is expensive

Civet or elephant? The most expensive coffee passes through the intestines of these animals.Kopi Luwak is coffee made from the feces of a cat-like animal, the Asian palm civet. Coffee cherries are fermented as they pass through the animal's digestive tract, after which they are harvested and sold. A half-kilogram bag of such grains in elite stores can cost up to $700.

Coffee is good for health...

Coffee is rich in antioxidants, which protect our cells from toxins, chemicals and inflammation.Earlier this year, a study published in the Annals of Internal Medicine found that drinking up to three cups of coffee a day reduced the risk of death from several key causes, particularly heart disease.

Researchers observed more than 500 thousand people in 10 European countries for 16 years.Other studies have examined whether coffee can reduce the risk of developing type 2 diabetes and fight neurodegenerative diseases such as dementia and Alzheimer's disease. But further research is required in these industries.

  • Coffee does more good than harm

The caffeine content of coffee also helps people increase energy levels and improve athletic performance.

...but in moderation

Because caffeine is a stimulant, it may pose a risk if consumed in excess. Pregnant women may want to limit their caffeine intake, as the substance has been linked to low birth weight and sometimes miscarriages.

The British Health Service recommends that pregnant women consume no more than 200 mg of caffeine per day. This is a little more than a cup of filter coffee or two cups of instant coffee.

...but most coffee per capita is consumed in Scandinavia

According to the International Coffee Organization, Finland consumes the most coffee per capita. Each resident of this country consumes an average of 12 kg of coffee annually. Next on the list are Norway (9.9 kg per capita), Iceland (9 kg), Denmark (8.7 kg) and Sweden (8.2 kg). Italians, who have made coffee an integral part of the Dolce Vita, consume 5.9 kg of coffee per capita annually.

Coffee vs tea: who wins?

Which drink is more popular - coffee or tea? The British Coffee Association says coffee is "the most popular drink in the world." Around 2 billion cups of coffee are consumed around the world every day. But it's not that simple.
